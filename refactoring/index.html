This graph maps investment relationships between companies in the defence supply chain and their investors.

Three views
  • Network — companies at the centre, investors orbiting around them. Links connect each investor to the companies it backed.
  • Bipartite — the same data in two parallel columns: investors on the left, companies on the right. Easier to trace a single investor's portfolio.
  • Projection — investors only. A link between two investors means they co-invested in at least one company. Thicker lines = more shared companies.
Shapes & colours
  • Circles = companies, coloured by sector
  • Squares = investors, coloured by type (fund / bank / government / institution)
  • The number inside a square is the investor's total portfolio count
  • A ★ on a circle means the company is also an investor
Interact

  • Drag nodes to rearrange; scroll to zoom
  • Companies / Investors — toggle either type on or off to focus on one side of the network
  • Search — type a name to dim unrelated nodes instantly
  • Lead only — keep only edges where the investor led the round
  • Hide isolated — remove nodes with no visible connections after other filters

0. Who we are

info.nodes is an Italian nonprofit organization, founded in 2019 by journalists and activists to experiment new solutions in the Italian civil society and media landscape.

We focus on social and climate justice, the impact of new technologies on human rights, and the protection of freedom of speech.

We produce investigations, connect tech-activists, researchers, and artists, develop innovative advocacy and communication formats to reach wider audiences. We also organize exhibitions, networking events, offer technical training for journalists and activists, and create investigative-based comms materials.

We love to work in collaboration with other organizations, because "no man is an island".

Project team: Davide Del Monte, Laura Carrer, Andrea Daniele Signorelli

In collaboration with:
Datapitch — An Italian company providing support in data analysis and data visualization, thanks to their experience in database development and management, multimedia content production, and the creation of digital tools.

1. Objective and scope

Identifying who truly holds the reins of autonomous weapons research and production, by reconstructing decision-making influence and investments across the entire supply chain.

Our ideal goal — sectors mapped by market cap:

Tech ≈ $15,000B Defence ≈ $1,000B Mining ≈ $900B

Identify the main investors and governance relationships across Tech / Defence / Mining — including Investment Funds (BlackRock, Vanguard Group, Fidelity Investments, State Street Global Advisors, JP Morgan) and Venture Capital (a16z, Sequoia Capital, Tiger Global Management, NEA, Lightspeed Venture Partners).

2. The 3 main focus
01
Start-up in Europe
02
Private investors in MoT
03
Public EU funding to MoT

We want to highlight the significant growth of the European startup sector dedicated to the development of autonomous systems and defense technologies. One of the key questions we are investigating is which funds, companies, or individuals are investing in the sector across multiple segments of the supply chain — from raw material extraction → development of AI-based technologies → production of armaments.

If an entity is present at every stage of the chain, their power increases considerably.

Regarding the startup sector in particular, we also want to understand the role played by European institutions in financially backing and thus promoting the development of this industrial sector.

4. The research

1. Identification of companies

We created a test list of companies based on market capitalization or economic valuation (for unlisted companies, such as startups). Additionally, we asked several Italian and international organizations to suggest names of companies they considered interesting for inclusion in the list.

The list is composed by:

  • 18 European start-up companies
  • 22 Global Tech companies
  • 65 Global Defence companies
  • 67 Global Mining companies

Enrichment of the list of companies with all the subjects (companies, universities, research centers) that received European funds (EDF, Horizon, etc…).

2. Data collection

In mid-February, we opened a one-month paid subscription on Crunchbase, one of the most well-known private databases for information on companies and investment funds. This subscription allowed us to upload our list of companies, view detailed information on them, and export the relevant data in CSV format.

* A direct API connection is not possible, as the cost exceeds $35,000 USD per year.

Datapitch developed a script to create a GitHub-hosted mini-site for info.nodes, listing every company previously identified with dedicated pages that organize their Wikipedia data according to a custom schema we designed.

3. Data analysis

Together with Datapitch, we developed code (using Claude) to analyze the collected data and create network analysis visualizations. Our core question is: 'Who is investing money in multiple companies and across multiple interconnected sectors?'

We created a GitHub repository to host the data analysis and network visualization. We want to make all our results accessible and free for all.

4. Research / Investigation

The research team (Davide, Laura, Andrea Daniele) will analyse and investigate the most interesting information and publish a report focused on the European start-up ecosystems, the main private investors in the MoT sector, and the stream of EU funds.

Possibility to investigate governance connections and relationships with public universities too.

Proposal for a follow-up: Hackathon with P.I. using the data collected by info.nodes and DataPitch.
